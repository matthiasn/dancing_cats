AZONTO  mean 5.30

Verdicts:
  - afrobeats: Beautifully grounded step-touch groove, but the wheel and the jab never leave the sternum — this reads as a cute chest-clutch bounce, not Azonto, and no Accra crowd would name the move.
  - movement: A grounded step-touch trapped under a frozen prayer-clasp — the steering and jab mimes that define Azonto never happen, so the move fails its own brief despite competent legwork.
  - director: Charming grounded footwork, but the Azonto never reaches the camera — both signature mimes die in a chest-clump and the loop flat-lines after its one beat-1 accent.
  - anatomy: A believable step-touch lower body wasted under arms welded to the sternum for the entire phrase — the wheel and jab vocabulary never articulates at shoulder, elbow, or wrist.
  - rigging: Solid step-touch base with clean torso/head continuity, but the arm system fails on every jab beat (stacked interpenetrating paws, slab-kinked sleeves) and the support foot measurably skates through bar 2 — not sign-off ready; note the strip delivers 24 cells, not the 32 frames specified.

Critiques:
  [afrobeats @f13] Bar 1's steering-wheel mime has no wheel: both fists stay clumped together at the sternum and merely cross at the wrists, so there is zero visible wheel diameter or rotation — the defining quote of the bar is illegible.
      FIX: Separate the fists to shoulder width in front of the chest (hand x roughly ±25-30) and rotate them around a shared center — left fist rises to brow height while the right drops to rib height, then swap over two beats with soft elbows; keep hands 'on the rim' for at least 3 of bar 1's 4 beats so the quote owns the bar.
  [afrobeats @f28] Bar 2's cross-body jab never extends and the idle paw is not chambered: at the beat-8 hit the jabbing fist stops at the opposite pec with the elbow still bent ~90°, never clearing the torso silhouette, and the off paw floats at chest height instead of sitting at the hip.
      FIX: Drive the jab to near-full elbow extension past the opposite shoulder line (fist visibly outside the body silhouette on the cross side) with a sharp easeOut snap on each of beats 5-8, and pin the idle paw as a fist at the iliac crest (hip height, elbow drawn back behind the ribline) for the whole bar.
  [afrobeats @f5] Signature-quote duty cycle is far too low and the in-between texture is wrong: between mimes the hands collapse into the same clenched-chest neutral, and the free foot swings into a high hopscotch-style knee lift instead of Azonto's ball-of-foot pivot/tap under the body.
      FIX: Fill inter-quote frames with the catalogued down-pointing loose-wrist hand circles instead of returning to the chest-clutch hold, and re-author the free foot to stay near the floor pivoting in-out on the ball (heel swivel each beat) against a stiffer planted support leg; add the head-turn toward the miming hand to sell each quote.
  [movement @f20] Bar 2 contains no cross-body jabs at all: both paws stay fused at the sternum for the entire bar, elbows never extend, no paw crosses the midline, and the idle paw never chambers at the hip — the move's named vocabulary is simply absent.
      FIX: Author explicit jab keys on beats 5-8: the firing paw extends across the midline to near-full elbow extension in one frame with a 2-3 frame recoil, the opposite paw pulls back to the hip crest, shoulders counter-rotate 15-20 degrees into each jab, and the head/gaze tracks the punching paw; alternate sides each beat.
  [movement @f3] The bar-1 steering-wheel mime is unreadable: the hands hold a static clasp with only a few pixels of drift, no hand-over-hand rotation, no arc — from the audience view it reads as praying, not driving.
      FIX: Place both fists on an imaginary wheel rim in front of the sternum and rotate the pair through alternating plus/minus 60-degree arcs (down-left on beat 1, down-right on beat 2), leading with alternating elbows, with a small head tilt and gaze shift into each turn to sell the mime.
  [movement @f5] The high leg-lift — the phrase's only accent — peaks a third of a beat after the downbeat, gets zero upper-body response (arms stay frozen mid-clasp), and drops into a dead instant plant, so the body reads as two disconnected halves and the accent dies.
      FIX: Retime the lift apex to land exactly on the beat, add counterbalance (opposite arm drops and opens past the hip with 2-3 frames of overshoot, torso side-bends over the standing leg), and give the landing a two-frame knee-fold settle instead of a hard plant — or replace the lift with a low toe-tap point, which is truer to Azonto step-touch vocabulary.

  [director @f19] Bar 2's cross-body jab never fires: the lunging stride promises a punch, but both paws remain clasped at the sternum and the idle paw is never chambered at the hip — the arms stay entirely inside the torso silhouette all bar.
      FIX: On each jab beat, snap one arm fully across the body past the far shoulder line (paw clearly outside the silhouette, ~2 frames out, 1 frame overshoot, ease back), pin the idle paw at the hip crest, and alternate sides per beat so the jab/chamber contrast reads.

      FIX: Distribute bend across the ribbon mesh with proper elbow weighting so extension reads shoulder-elbow-wrist, round the sleeve terminal cap with the cuff/paw placed past the sleeve end, and offset the tail curve to clear the arm silhouette.
