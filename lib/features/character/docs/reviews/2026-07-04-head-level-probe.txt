Head-level pixel-track probe — 2026-07-04
Resolves the R12 pouncingCat 4-vs-1 split (see 2026-07-03-panel-round12.txt).
Probe: test/features/character/runtime/pouncing_cat_head_probe_test.dart

== The question ==
pouncingCat's premise is "a dead-level head over a gliding base." R12's five
raters split on the directly falsifiable head-level claim:
- anatomy (8.0): "a genuinely dead-level head over the gliding legs —
  professional-grade."
- movement (7.0): measured "the head's vertical position swings ~54-59px...
  not decoupled from the torso at all."
- director (6.5) / afrobeats (6.5): "the head visibly bobs with the body."
R12 asked for a direct pixel-track of the RENDERED head position before
trusting either side (the technique that cracked the R10 azonto/sekem
"solved-rotation-doesn't-render" mysteries).

== Method ==
Track scene.frameAt(...).world[bone].ty across a full pouncingCat loop (240
samples). That map is the resolved world-space origin the renderer actually
sees — AFTER foot stabilization and the head-level counter (_rigidHeadWorld),
the same layer TemporalMotionAnalyzer probes. At the lead's render scale
(~0.55) one world unit ~= one rendered pixel.

== Verdict: the four are right. The head is NOT level. ==
pouncingCat rendered head, measured:
  head Y swing    = 99.5 world-units peak-to-peak = 37.3% of body height
                  = ~55px at lead scale 0.55  (dead on movement's "54-59px")
  hips Y swing    = 74.5 units
  head/hips coupling = 1.34  (the head moves MORE than the hips it rides)
  neck 113 / ear_tip 99 — the whole head group swings the same third-of-body.

Coupling 1.34 means the head is not decoupled at all; it slightly AMPLIFIES
the body's vertical travel. There is no "dead-level head" on the vertical axis.

== Why anatomy said "dead-level" and was still self-consistent ==
This is the same data->render gap R11 documented, not a rater error. pouncingCat
is AUTHORED level: danceHeadBobScale: 0, and flat local neck/head SineChannels
(cat_in_suit.dart) — "held flat, no inherited dance nod." Read as joint
mechanics / authored data, the head IS level, and anatomy read that layer.
But the head bone is parented above hips->torso->neck. The genuine pounce
CROUCH (knees/hips/torso drop) is real body geometry that propagates straight
up the chain into the head. The head-level system
(_danceHeadVerticalCounter) only cancels the small rootDy *groove* term — a
light ~0.24 fraction, clamped [-20, 4] — and never touches the crouch. Flat
LOCAL head channels cannot level a head whose PARENT drops. So authored-level
+ inherited-crouch = a head that bobs a third of the figure's height.

== It is catalogue-wide, not a pouncingCat quirk ==
head/hips vertical coupling, all six clips (probe table):
  shaku 1.27 (12.7% bodyH) | zanku 1.10 (23.2%) | azonto 1.44 (16.6%)
  sekem 0.98 (19.2%) | buga 1.22 (44.4%, worst) | pouncingCat 1.34 (37.3%)
Every clip couples >= 0.98: the head rides the body's vertical fully or more.
"Dead-level head over a gliding base" is a premise NO catalogue move currently
implements on the vertical axis. pouncingCat is just where a rater claimed it.

== Secondary (afrobeats "feet pinned in one static lunge") — needs its own look ==
Over the loop, feet travel ~89/93 units in X and hips glide ~48 units — so the
base is not frozen. But peak-to-peak can't distinguish a smooth GLIDE from a
plant-shift-plant STEP under the support-foot anchor (strength 0.88). The
"glides vs pinned" read is a separate question the head probe doesn't settle;
flag for a dedicated foot/base-path probe if pursued.

== Fix ATTEMPTED and rejected: translation-based leveling opens the throat ==
Tried the obvious runtime fix — a crouch/arc-aware vertical counter driven by
the head's REAL resolved deviation from its per-clip mean (not rootDy), so it
folds in the spine arc. Two variants:
  1. head-only counter, join-clamped to keep the head 6..22px above the neck.
     Result: coupling barely moved (1.34 -> 1.31). The head is GLUED to the
     neck, and the neck itself over-travels (113px, see above) — so leveling the
     head alone is capped by the neck's own swing.
  2. TWO-STAGE spine leveler (level the neck, then the head), each clamped to its
     joint's NATURAL vertical-gap envelope so bone gaps provably never exceed the
     shipping rig. NUMERICALLY this looked great: coupling 1.34 -> 1.05, neck
     swing 113 -> 82, and BOTH joints got TIGHTER than natural (head-neck range
     15.1 -> 3.8, neck-torso 46.7 -> 13.2).
But the RENDER (the whole point of the probe) vetoes it. In the crouch frames
the head visibly lifts off the collar — a real gap opens between the chin and
the collar/tie, the head reads as detached. See:
  assets/pounce-headlevel-throatgap-cell1.png   (tight throat zoom, before/after)
  assets/pounce-headlevel-throatgap-crouch.png  (three crouch cells)
Why the numeric join-clamp did NOT save it: the collar/tie/shirt bones hang off
the CHEST/torso, not the neck, so they stay down with the body while the leveled
neck+head lift. Bone-gap-within-envelope is necessary but NOT sufficient for
visual join integrity. Strength is not the lever either — the natural-gap clamp
binds, so 0.5 and 1.0 give the identical result. Settle-only (push the head DOWN
at its high extreme, never lift) is throat-safe but flattens the pounce's upward
accent — wrong for a POUNCE. Change reverted; only the probe + this note remain.

== The real fix (panel-gated) ==
Any pure TRANSLATION of the head/neck trades the bob for a detached head. To
hold the head level while it stays attached, the neck must BEND/shorten, not
translate: re-angle (or foreshorten) the neck so the skull's absolute Y holds
while the neck BASE stays welded to the collar through the crouch. Alternatively
(choreography, not rig): make the pounce crouch shallower or absorb more of it in
the knees/hips so the CHEST — and with it the head — doesn't drop as far, joints
intact. Both need render iteration + the focused 3-expert re-panel R12 called
for; the throat is exactly the thing that must be judged on rendered frames, not
metrics. Reproduce/measure any candidate with the probe test.

== FIX SHIPPED: two-stage spine leveler + throat bridge + deep-crouch ease
The runtime fix now lives in `_spineLevelShifts` / `_spineLevelPlan`
(character_scene.dart). Three parts:
  1. TWO-STAGE LEVELER: level the neck toward its per-clip mean height, then the
     head on top, each clamped to that joint's NATURAL vertical-gap envelope so a
     joint never separates more than it already does un-leveled (coupling alone:
     1.34 -> 1.05, joints TIGHTER than natural).
  2. THROAT BRIDGE: the collar/tie/shirt bones hang off the chest, not the neck,
     so a bare leveler opens an orange gap under the chin. Lift the bridge fabric
     0.7x the neck shift so the collar follows the throat up (no detach gap).
  3. DEEP-CROUCH EASE: hold-frames pin level, but past an ABSOLUTE crouch-depth
     deadzone the leveling eases off so the head DIPS a little on the pounce
     bottom — restores weight and kills the "tubey neck" the panel flagged. Gated
     on absolute depth (not per-clip) so a shallow-crouch clip (shaku) gets ~no
     ease and its rigid-skull smoothness gate still holds.
Post-fix pounce: coupling ~1.18, head-swing ~88 (from 1.34/99.5), head-neck gap
held tight and never crossing below the neck, hold-frame head-top near-constant.

3-EXPERT PANEL (2026-07-04, per R12 scoping: movement + rigging + performance)
picked the candidate UNANIMOUSLY over the bobbing baseline:
  biomechanics 7->8 | rigging 8->8 | performance 6->8 (all: variant B better)
Performance dir pixel-measured head-top: candidate pins holds at 36-37px vs the

The one residual they flagged — a slightly tubey neck on the 2 deepest frames —
